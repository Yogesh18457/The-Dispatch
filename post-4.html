After Historic 4-0 Whitewash, Questions Mount Over India's T20 Coaching and Selection

By Yogesh · July 19, 2026

India's T20I team has hit a low point rarely seen in the format's history, losing a bilateral series 4-0 to England — the first time India has ever been swept 4-0 in T20Is — and extending their winless run to seven matches following an earlier 2-0 series loss to Ireland.

The series had a brutal middle chapter: in the third match at Trent Bridge, India was bowled out for just 76 runs, their second-lowest T20I total ever, as England pacers Jofra Archer and Josh Tongue tore through the batting order. The finale in Southampton was no better, with England chasing down a target built on a record 233-run stand between Jos Buttler (131 off 64 balls) and Harry Brook (95 not out) — the highest partnership England has recorded in the format.

For new T20 captain Shreyas Iyer, who took over from Suryakumar Yadav, the results mean he remains winless in his first seven matches in charge, an unwanted record to begin a captaincy tenure.

The scale of the losses has put both head coach Gautam Gambhir and the national selectors under scrutiny. The BCCI is expected to hold a formal review of the team's performance, though board secretary Devajit Saikia has said no "knee-jerk" decisions will be made, and Gambhir's contract runs until 2027. Still, reports suggest tension has been building inside the camp since India's Test series loss to South Africa at home, with friction reported between the selection committee and coaching staff.

Fans haven't been as measured — social media reaction after the series finale was sharply critical of both team selection and tactical decisions, with many calling for changes to the coaching set-up rather than the playing squad itself.

India's next assignment is a three-match T20I series in Zimbabwe from July 23–26, giving Iyer an early chance to open his account as captain away from the intensity of an England tour.
