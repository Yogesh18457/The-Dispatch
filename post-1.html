NEET Protests Enter Parliament March Phase as Wangchuk Hospitalised, Results Now Out

By Yogesh · Jul 19, 2026

A protest movement that began as satirical Instagram commentary has grown into one of the more closely watched standoffs in Delhi this summer, as the Cockroach Janta Party (CJP) prepares for what is being called a historic march to Parliament.

The movement, initially dismissed by mainstream media as an internet phenomenon, has mobilized thousands of NEET aspirants and their families across India. What started as witty social media posts criticizing NEET examination procedures has transformed into organized political demonstrations demanding systemic reforms.

Wangchuk's Hospitalization Escalates Crisis

Prominent activist and movement figurehead has been hospitalized amid the intensifying standoff. Sources close to the movement indicate his condition is being closely monitored by supporters and family members. The hospitalization has raised concerns about police action and medical treatment of protesters.

NEET Results Released Amid Controversy

The National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) results have been announced, adding urgency to the movement. The release has reignited debates about the examination's fairness, with protesters pointing to statistical anomalies and demanding a comprehensive inquiry into the testing procedures.

Parliament March Planned

The Cockroach Janta Party has announced plans for a mass march to Parliament, aiming to present their demands directly to lawmakers. Organizers are expecting participation from multiple states and have coordinated with various civil society groups.

Key Demands

  • Transparency in NEET examination procedures
  • Independent audit of previous examination cycles
  • Reform of medical entrance test protocols
  • Direct engagement with Parliament on student concerns

The movement represents a significant challenge to the current examination system and highlights growing frustration among aspirants and their families with standardized testing protocols in India.
