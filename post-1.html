NEET Protests Enter Parliament March Phase as Wangchuk Hospitalised, Results Now Out

By Yogesh · July 19, 2026

A protest movement that began as satirical Instagram commentary has grown into one of the more closely watched standoffs in Delhi this summer, as the Cockroach Janta Party (CJP) prepares to march on Parliament on July 20, just a day after activist Sonam Wangchuk was forcibly hospitalised following three weeks of hunger strike. CJP, founded by Abhijeet Dipke, began as an online satirical movement before organizing physical protests at Jantar Mantar starting June 20. The group has since held demonstrations in multiple locations before consolidating at Jantar Mantar, demanding the resignation of Union Education Minister Dharmendra Pradhan over April's NEET-UG paper leak and subsequent exam cancellation. Alongside the resignation demand, CJP has pushed for compensation for the families of students who died by suicide, which the group links to the stress of the exam's cancellation and the uncertainty that followed. Dipke wrote to Prime Minister Modi seeking ₹1 crore per family and a transparent verification process for the claims. The group's own count of such deaths has run into double digits, though that figure comes from CJP and news reporting rather than an official government tally. The movement gained national attention when Sonam Wangchuk joined with an indefinite hunger strike on June 28. On July 18, Delhi Police removed him from the protest site and took him to Safdarjung Hospital, citing Delhi High Court orders and medical advice over his deteriorating condition. Police said a brief scuffle broke out as some protesters tried to stop the move; officials maintain they acted only to protect Wangchuk's health. The episode has split political reaction rather than unifying it. AAP's Arvind Kejriwal argued the government should have engaged Wangchuk directly instead of removing him. Azad Samaj Party chief Chandra Shekhar Azad called the action an assault on democratic values. At the same time, some voices — including former Shiv Sena (UBT) MP Priyanka Chaturvedi — have criticised CJP itself, arguing the movement shouldn't use Wangchuk's health as political leverage. CJP has called for supporters to gather peacefully at Jantar Mantar on July 20 and march toward Parliament, timed to the opening of the Monsoon Session, to press its demands: Pradhan's resignation, reform of the exam system, and compensation for affected families. Meanwhile, the exam process itself has moved forward. The NTA conducted the re-scheduled NEET-UG exam on June 21 and declared results on July 16 — roughly 20 lakh candidates appeared, with 11.21 lakh qualifying. Counselling for medical college admissions is now underway alongside the protests over how the original controversy was handled.
